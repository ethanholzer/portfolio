Case Study · Step · Growth Design

Personalizing signup to increase trust and conversion

At Step, I redesigned the signup flow by introducing personalization and trust-building moments—challenging the assumption that fewer steps always improve conversion.

+102% KYC Completion
+51% First Transaction Conversion
Role
Lead Product Designer & Product Manager (Growth)
Scope
Strategy, experimentation, execution, stakeholder & leadership alignment
Problem

Users dropped off before reaching product value

Step's signup flow saw significant drop-off, especially when users were asked to verify their identity (KYC). Users were hesitant to provide sensitive information like their SSN before understanding the product's value.

  • Major drop-off before KYC submission
  • Additional attrition scattered across the flow
  • Low trust at the most critical moment

Initial assumption

Leadership believed the issue was too much friction.

  • Too many steps in the flow
  • Flow should be shorter
  • Remove or combine screens

This framing led to optimizations in the wrong direction.

Photos from a meeting I facilitated with core leadership to align on solving the right problems.

My approach

The issue wasn't length — it was lack of trust

Based on prior research and user interviews, I believed users weren't motivated to provide sensitive information because they didn't yet understand the product's value.

  • Users wanted to "try the app first"
  • Trust wasn't established before asking for sensitive data
  • Motivation was unclear at the KYC moment
Experiment

Testing personalization with an interest selector

I introduced a skippable screen asking users what they wanted to do first — to test whether personalization could increase engagement and trust.

The hypothesis: if users could express intent early, they'd be more motivated to complete the steps that followed — including KYC.

Results
  • Slight increase in KYC conversion
  • 0% skip rate — full engagement
  • Clear signal of user intent and trust

The 0% skip rate was the real finding. Users didn't want to skip — they wanted to tell us who they were.

Captures user intent Personalizes next steps
Solution

Personalizing the moment that matters most

Based on the experiment, I expanded personalization into the KYC step — tailoring messaging to each user's selected goal and connecting their motivation directly to identity verification.

Before — Generic
We need to verify your identity
To comply with regulations, we need your Social Security Number. This is required by law.
After — Personalized

Connecting user motivation to identity verification increased trust — and completion.

Challenges

Challenging the "less is more" mindset

There was strong skepticism around adding more steps. Alignment required data, framing, and patience.

i

Justifying added screens

Needed to demonstrate through experiment data that an additional step could net-improve conversion, not just add friction.

ii

Aligning stakeholders

Used the 0% skip rate and KYC uplift from the pilot to shift the conversation from "fewer steps" to "right steps."

iii

Framing around outcomes

Anchored every design decision to MTU and KYC completion — the metrics leadership cared about most.

Results
+102% Increase in KYC completion
+51% Increase in first transaction conversion
Takeaways

What I learned

01

Adding steps can improve conversion when they build trust rather than create friction.

02

Personalization increases motivation at critical moments — especially when asking for sensitive data.

03

Small, focused experiments can unlock major product shifts and create stakeholder alignment.
